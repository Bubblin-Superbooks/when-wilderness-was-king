Chapter I

A Message From The West

SURELY it was no longers ago than yesterday. I had left the scythe lying at the edge of the long grass, and gone up through the rows of nodding Indian corn to the house, seeking a draught of cool water from thse spring. It was hot in the July sunshine; the thick forest on every side intercepted the breeze, and I had been at work for some hours.
